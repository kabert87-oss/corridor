When a Neighbour Builds a Continent's Gateway: Ethiopia's Bishoftu Gambit

Ethiopia broke ground on a $12.5 billion airport designed to handle 110 million passengers annually — Africa's largest. Addis Ababa is not building for today's demand. It is engineering tomorrow's corridor hierarchy. JKIA processed 8.93 million passengers in 2025 against a designed capacity of 7.5 million. Ethiopian Airlines added 75,300 seats in March 2026. Kenya Airways contracted by 1.3 percent in the same period. The Corridor Index is shifting. The Corridor analyses what Nairobi must do before the window closes.
