The $2 Billion Structural Leak: Why East Africa's Tourism Economy Fails Its Own People

Africa is not short of tourism revenue. It is short of policy architecture that converts mobility into development. Kenya recorded over $2 billion in tourism receipts in 2023, yet the structural reality beneath these numbers tells a different story. Tourism revenue flows outward, to internationally owned hotel groups, to foreign headquartered tour operators, to intermediaries who capture margin before a single dollar reaches the community that made the journey worth taking.
