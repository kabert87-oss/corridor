When the Debt Comes Due, Tourism Pays the Price

Kenya's IMF programme collapsed after failing 11 of 16 performance conditions, with $850 million withheld. Debt servicing now consumes more than 70 percent of ordinary revenue. Tourism is generating record foreign exchange — Ksh560 billion projected for 2026 — yet receives less than 4 percent in public reinvestment. The Corridor analyses the fiscal trap constraining East Africa's most productive sector and the structural instrument that can break it.
