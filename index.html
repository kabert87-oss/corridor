When China Opens the Door,
East Africa Must Decide What to Sell

China has announced zero tariffs for 53 African countries effective May 2026. Chinese Vice President Han Zheng visited Nairobi this week. Kenya is targeting one million Chinese tourists. The diplomatic moment is real. The Sovereign Tourism Architecture to convert it is not yet built.

China will forgo $1.4 billion in tariff revenue to open its market to East Africa. Han Zheng attended the departure of the first zero-tariff freight train from the SGR dispatch centre in Nairobi. Kenya recorded 105,000 Chinese tourists in 2025 against a target of one million. The Corridor analyses three structural vulnerabilities in East Africa's China tourism relationship and the interventions that cannot wait for the next FOCAC summit.
