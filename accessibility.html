Accessibility & CC

Our commitment to accessible design and Creative Commons licensing for The Corridor content.

Last updated: March 2026

Our Commitment to Accessibility

The Corridor is committed to ensuring that our publication is accessible to all readers, including those with disabilities. We strive to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA.

Accessibility Features

  • Semantic HTML structure for screen reader compatibility
  • Sufficient colour contrast ratios across all page elements
  • Keyboard navigable interface throughout the website
  • Descriptive link text and image alt attributes
  • Responsive design that works across all screen sizes and devices
  • Readable typography with clear visual hierarchy

Creative Commons Licensing

The analysis, frameworks and original concepts published in The Corridor are the intellectual property of The Corridor. Brief excerpts may be quoted for educational or commentary purposes with full attribution and a link to the original article.

Original analytical frameworks introduced by The Corridor, including the Sovereign Tourism Architecture and the Displacement Dividend, may be referenced and cited in academic or professional contexts provided The Corridor is credited as the originating source.

Known Limitations

We are continuously working to improve accessibility across the site. If you encounter any accessibility barriers, please let us know so we can address them promptly.

Feedback and Assistance

If you experience any difficulty accessing content on The Corridor, or if you require content in an alternative format, please contact us and we will do our best to assist you.
