About The Corridor

The only weekly publication at the intersection of global geopolitics and African tourism strategy.

Last updated: March 2026

What is The Corridor?

The Corridor is a weekly geopolitical tourism intelligence publication focused on Africa. Published every Monday from Nairobi, Kenya, it analyses the intersection of global politics, economics, conflict, climate and diplomacy with African tourism strategy.

The Corridor exists because tourism in Africa does not operate in isolation. It operates within a global system shaped by power politics, exchange rates, air connectivity, regulatory regimes and shifting demand patterns. Every week, global events alter the trajectory of travel flows. The Corridor distills those shifts into concise, executive-level intelligence for the decision-makers shaping Africa's tourism future.

The Six Lenses

Every issue is structured around one of six analytical lenses: Conflict and Displacement, Economics and Currency, Connectivity and Aviation, Policy and Governance, Climate and Environment, and Diplomacy and Trade. Each lens provides a distinct analytical angle on how global events reshape African tourism flows.

Who Reads The Corridor?

The Corridor is written for tourism ministers and government officials, national tourism boards, hotel groups and safari operators, development finance institutions, investors, and international relations scholars. It is designed for decision-makers who need to understand the geopolitical forces shaping African tourism strategy.

The Founder

The Corridor is founded by a Nairobi-based tourism practitioner and international relations scholar with thirteen years of industry experience across East Africa. The publication combines tourism practice with geopolitical analysis to deliver intelligence that is both analytically rigorous and immediately actionable.

Publishing Schedule

The Corridor publishes every Monday. Issues are available on this website and delivered free to subscribers via Substack at corridorafrica.substack.com.
