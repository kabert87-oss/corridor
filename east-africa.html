Latest Issue · Issue 005 · 30 March 2026 · Climate & Environment

When the Migration
Loses Its Calendar

East Africa's most commercially valuable tourism product is becoming structurally unreliable. Climate disruption from above. Luxury infrastructure dismantling it from below. The policy response has been neither coordinated nor proportionate.

The Mara-Loita wildebeest migration has lost 90 percent of its footprint since 2020. ICPAC has issued a live flood warning covering the exact circuits that drive peak season revenue. A temperature rise of 4.8 to 5.8 degrees Celsius over six decades is making the Migration's calendar structurally unreliable. The Corridor analyses the sovereign economic risk hiding in plain sight and the three moves East Africa cannot defer.
