💱 Economics & Currency · Issue #3

When the Debt Comes Due, Tourism Pays the Price

Kenya's IMF programme collapsed after failing 11 of 16 performance conditions, with $850 million withheld. An IMF team arrived in Nairobi on 24 February 2026 to negotiate a new programme. Debt servicing now consumes more than 70 percent of ordinary revenue, leaving less than 4 percent of tourism earnings reinvested in the sector.

Tourism is generating record foreign exchange — Ksh560 billion projected for 2026 — yet the fiscal architecture ensures it cannot fund its own growth. The Corridor introduces the Tourism Foreign Exchange Sovereign Reserve framework and sets out what Kenya's negotiating team must secure in Washington in April.
