Who We Are and How to Contact Us

The Corridor is an independent weekly geopolitical tourism intelligence publication published from Nairobi, Kenya. The data controller responsible for your personal information is The Corridor, operated by The Corridor Editorial Team.

For all privacy-related matters, data subject requests and questions about this policy, contact us directly at thecorridorafrica@gmail.com. All privacy enquiries are acknowledged within 5 business days and fully resolved within 30 calendar days.

Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 GDPR Aligned CCPA Aware ICO Registered: Not Required (Kenya-based controller)
02

What Data We Collect

The Corridor operates on a minimal data collection principle. We collect only what is necessary to deliver the publication and understand how readers engage with it. The following table sets out every category of data we collect, where it comes from and why.

Data Category Source Purpose Controller
Email address Provided voluntarily on subscription via Substack Delivery of the weekly publication Substack Inc. (processed on our behalf)
Subscription preferences Set by the subscriber within Substack Personalising delivery and notification settings Substack Inc.
Anonymised usage data Automatically collected via web analytics on thecorridorafrica.com Understanding content engagement, page performance and referral sources The Corridor (via analytics provider)

Cookie data Automatically collected on site visit Essential site functionality and anonymised analytics The Corridor (see Section 06)

We do not collect: payment information, government identification, sensitive personal data as defined under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 or GDPR, location data beyond country-level anonymised analytics, or any data from children under the age of 16.

04

Legal Basis for Processing

The Corridor processes personal data under the following legal bases, as defined by the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and consistent with GDPR Article 6:

Consent. Where you have subscribed to The Corridor via Substack, you have provided explicit consent for us to send you the weekly publication. You may withdraw consent at any time by unsubscribing using the link in any email we send. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.

Legitimate interests. We process anonymised analytics data and contact enquiry data on the basis of legitimate interests, specifically understanding how our publication is read and responding to professional enquiries. We have assessed that these interests do not override your rights and freedoms.

Legal obligation. In limited circumstances we may be required to process or disclose data to comply with a legal obligation under Kenyan law or applicable international law.

06

Cookies and Analytics

The Corridor uses cookies on thecorridorafrica.com for two purposes only: essential site functionality and anonymised analytics. We do not use advertising cookies, tracking cookies or any cookie that identifies you personally.

Essential cookies are required for the website to function correctly. They cannot be disabled without affecting site functionality. No personal data is collected through essential cookies.

Analytics cookies collect anonymised data about how visitors engage with the site, including pages visited, time on page and referral sources. This data cannot be used to identify individual visitors. It is used solely to improve the quality and relevance of The Corridor's content.

You may manage your cookie preferences at any time via our Cookie Policy page. For full details on the cookies used and how to disable them, please refer to that page.

07

Data Retention

The Corridor retains personal data only for as long as it is necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.

Subscriber data is retained for the duration of your active subscription to The Corridor via Substack. Upon unsubscription, your email address and associated data are deleted from Substack's active subscriber list in accordance with Substack's data deletion procedures. You may request confirmation of deletion at any time.

Contact enquiry data is retained for a maximum of 24 months from the date of the most recent communication, unless an ongoing professional relationship requires longer retention, in which case data is retained only for the duration of that relationship.

Anonymised analytics data does not contain personal information and is retained indefinitely for the purpose of understanding long-term content performance trends.

08

Your Rights

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and, where applicable, the General Data Protection Regulation, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data. All requests are responded to within 30 calendar days at no charge.

Right of Access
You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you and information about how it is processed.
Right to Rectification
You may request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.
Right to Erasure
You may request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.
Right to Restrict Processing
You may request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances, for example while a complaint is being investigated.
Right to Data Portability
You may request a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format for transfer to another service.
Right to Object
You may object to processing based on legitimate interests. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.
Right to Withdraw Consent
Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect prior lawful processing.
Right to Lodge a Complaint
You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner of Kenya or your local supervisory authority.

Office of the Data Protection Commissioner, Kenya: You may contact the ODPC at www.odpc.go.ke if you believe your data protection rights have been infringed and you are not satisfied with our response to your complaint.

09

International Data Transfers

The Corridor is operated from Kenya. When you subscribe via Substack, your data is processed by Substack Inc., a company incorporated in the United States. This constitutes an international transfer of personal data outside Kenya.

Substack participates in data transfer frameworks designed to ensure adequate protection for personal data transferred internationally. Where you are located in the European Economic Area, transfers to Substack are governed by Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission. For full details on Substack's international transfer mechanisms, please refer to Substack's privacy policy at substack.com/privacy.

The Corridor does not otherwise transfer your personal data outside Kenya except as described in Section 05 of this policy.

10

Children's Privacy

The Corridor is a professional policy intelligence publication intended for adult readers, policymakers and industry professionals. It is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under the age of 16.

If you believe that a child under 16 has provided personal data to The Corridor, please contact us immediately at thecorridorafrica@gmail.com and we will take immediate steps to delete that data.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. All previous versions are available on request by contacting thecorridorafrica@gmail.com.
