The Publication

Intelligence built for the people shaping Africa's tourism future

The Corridor is a weekly geopolitical tourism intelligence publication focused on Africa. Published every Monday, it analyses the intersection of global politics, economics, conflict, climate, connectivity and diplomacy with African tourism strategy, at Oxford postgraduate analytical standard, using proprietary frameworks developed specifically for this purpose.

Tourism in Africa does not operate in isolation. It operates within a global system shaped by power politics, exchange rates, air corridor hierarchies, regulatory regimes and shifting demand patterns. Every week, global events alter the trajectory of travel flows across the continent. Most of that analysis happens elsewhere, framed through lenses that position Africa as a recipient of global forces rather than a sovereign actor within them.

The Corridor exists to correct that framing. Every issue approaches Africa as a strategic actor with agency, leverage and the capacity to make deliberate choices about its position within the global tourism system. The analysis is interpretive, not informational. The conclusions are actionable, not observational.

"Africa does not have a tourism visibility problem. It has a strategic urgency problem. The world is looking. The question is whether Africa is ready to be found."

The Corridor, Issue 002 · March 2026

Proprietary Frameworks

Original analytical tools, built here, first applied here

The Corridor does not borrow the language of tourism marketing or development economics. It has developed its own analytical frameworks, introduced and reinforced across every issue, that give decision-makers a precise vocabulary for understanding how the global tourism system operates and where Africa's strategic leverage lies.

Framework 01
Sovereign Tourism Architecture
The integrated system through which a state deploys its carrier, infrastructure and policy as a single coordinated investment vehicle to engineer long-term corridor control. Ethiopia's Bishoftu strategy is its most advanced current continental expression.
Framework 02
Displacement Dividend
The economic value transferred to alternative destinations when a major competitor's tourism infrastructure collapses. The destination that moves fastest to absorb displaced demand captures structural market share that persists long after the original crisis resolves.
Framework 03
Mobility Corridors
The physical and regulatory infrastructure through which tourism flows move between source markets and destinations. Corridor architecture determines which cities capture transit value, which destinations are accessible to premium source markets and which are structurally excluded.
Framework 04
Corridor Index
A composite measure of a gateway's relative accessibility, routing centrality and hub attractiveness within Africa's aviation system. The Index is not static. It shifts with capacity decisions, infrastructure investment and bilateral air services agreements made by competing corridor actors.

Tourism in Africa operates within a global system shaped by power politics, exchange rates, air connectivity, regulatory regimes and shifting demand patterns. For more than a decade, I witnessed policy and investment decisions made without integrated strategic intelligence. Analysis was abundant. Strategic intelligence, the kind that tells a tourism minister or an airline executive not just what happened but what it means and what to do, was absent.

The Corridor exists to fill that gap. It combines thirteen years of tourism practice across Africa with international relations scholarship to produce analysis that is both analytically rigorous and immediately actionable. Every issue is built on the conviction that Africa is a strategic actor with the agency, leverage and intellectual capacity to shape its own position within the global tourism economy.

The frameworks in this publication — Sovereign Tourism Architecture, Displacement Dividend, Mobility Corridors and Corridor Index — are not borrowed from the development economics literature. They were developed here, applied here, and tested against the actual decisions being made by the governments, carriers and institutions that determine where tourism capital flows on this continent.
